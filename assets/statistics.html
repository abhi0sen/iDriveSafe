Number of Casualties due to Certain Speed Limit

it is generally known that higher speeds increase the risk and severity of accidents. The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that a 5% increase in average speed can result in a 10% increase in fatal crashes. Furthermore, the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) states that speeding was a factor in 26% of all traffic fatalities in 2019 in the United States

Acccident Severity based on Light Condition

According to a report by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), fatalities from traffic accidents are three times higher at night compared to during the day. Additionally, the NHTSA reports that drivers are more likely to be involved in fatal accidents during dawn and dusk, as well as during inclement weather.

No of Casualties based on Road Type

According to data from the World Health Organization (WHO), the risk of a fatal accident is higher on high-speed roads such as highways, as well as on rural and secondary roads with poor infrastructure. This is due to factors such as high speeds, lack of safety features, and reduced access to medical care in case of an accident.
